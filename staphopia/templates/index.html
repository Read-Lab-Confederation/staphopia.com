About Staphopia

Staphopia is a database of information about the genomes of the bacterial pathogen Staphylococcus aureus. Life-threatening Methicillin-resistant S. aureus (MRSA) infections strike across our society, both in the community and hospital settings. In the past 3-4 years whole genome shotgun sequencing of clinical isolates has become common, as technology improvements have reduced the cost of producing genetic data.

The introduction of 'bench top' instruments, such as the Illumina MiSeq, has accelerated this trend. Staphopia aims to provide rapid analysis of whole-genome data with the aim of producing useful S. aureus specific results, such as genotype, antibiotic resistance and virulence gene profiles. Staphopia was designed to be used by people not expert in genomics.

We are currently developing the beta version of the website. In later updates we will allow users to submit their own data to our pipeline as well as a number of additional features. The main genomes page (currently displaying the results of analysis of more than 24,000 publicly available S. aureus projects downloaded from the NCBI SRA website) allows users to search the genomes based on attributes such as sequence type.

Clicking on a genome entry brings up an individual page, allowing the user to explore the attributes predicted by our pipeline.

Staphopia was created by Robert Petit and Tim Read at Emory University School of Medicine in Atlanta Georgia, USA.
